Semi-lively colors

When colors aabsorb into the canvas, it means that it has been primed only once, & for this reason they become matte without being shiny. But this is the best way, as long as you re-coat it twice, for in this manner, the colors, having more body, do not die & are all the more beautiful for it, especially azure ,lake & those do not have body. But those who want to rush their painting prime twice on the first go, to be done with it. Thus the colors do not absorb, also they will not last as long. The second layer of these does not absorb & remains shiny.

Painting large figures

You need to put your charcoal at the tip of a stick such that you make your first drawing from a distance because up close, you would not be able to judge proportions as well as from a certain & sufficient distance. Also, when you paint up close, hold your charcoal at the farthest end, & practically with your fingertips. For in this way you will acquire an artist's l touch & give your hand effortless facility.

Glazing

One usually glazes with colors which do not have body, such as lake & verdigris. However, to use other kinds, one mixes in plenty of calcined & crushed cristallin, which also has no body & makes lightens their thickness.

White and black

It is good that an apprentice should work with white & black for two or three years to become an artist.

Lead white

It does not die & has a lot of body.

After nature

There are some who paint after nature on oiled paper, & if they do not finish in one go, they put their paper & unfinished picture in water so that it shall not dry.
Illuminators painting on paper temper their colors with gum & mix in a little soap to make them run better.
Painters must all learn how to draw after nature, for to them it is travel provisions & revenue & sure means of earning their passage when travelling across the lands with only their cocon.
