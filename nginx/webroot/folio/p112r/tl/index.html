To temper the sand

You should have: a large basin full of water to dip the molds into in order to open them, a tankard full of common water, a dish to temper the sand, a small wooden spoon to pick up the mixed sand in the dish.
To make handles for your large oil brushes, if you do not have large enough feather pipes, take two of them, & melt them & join them together.

Molding en noyau. Mixing of sand

Once you have reheated your sand, that is the plaster, the stone alum & the brick, & once you have finely filtered them through a cloth sieve, except for the stone alum which should be ground in a mortar.

Killing the animal to mold

You must have been careful not to hurt it while capturing it. If you have a stock of animals and you wish to keep them for a long time, place it in a glass bottle with bran or in a large barrel half full of damp & fresh earth. Give them frogs to eat, live & and not dead, because should you keep them without feeding them, they would wither & become emaciated, & their skin would become saggy and wrinkled. Therefore, the sooner after its capture you mold it, the better it will come out. And kill it in the following manner: place it in a clean bottle or vessel so that the animal does not carry any dust; fill the bottle with enough good vinegar & urine so as to cover the animal. Shake the bottle & torment the animal inside there until it is dead which should take an hour or half an hour. But if you are in a hurry
To know if the animal is quite dead, take it out of the vinegar and take it by the tail. If it moves its tail, do not mold it because it still has feelings, & when pricking its members, it would remove itself & harm the mold.
Water snakes do not bite much.
