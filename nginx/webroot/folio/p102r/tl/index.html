For the furnace

A coffer with a wide stone of ten square pousses, and a thickness of one pied, pierced in the middle2 boxes or earthenware pots with a lid2 small pipes of white iron to evacuate the smoke from the furnaceTurnipseed oil for the lamps to heat the furnaceA fire-steel

Royal cement

One pound & a half of vitriolAs much saltpeterAs much rock alum

Glass vessel

The glass vessel must be either pear-shaped or round pyramid-shaped, of the same thickness as the small back of a knife, round on the bottom without folding over on itself, like flasks, its opening of such a size that a Dutch feather can just about get in, & with a lip at the end, in this way of the height of seven fingers' width.
