Plaster mold for wax

When your plaster mold is done and dried, be sure that your mold comes off cleanly because it sometimes happens that the animal, having been wounded or having lost weight or having withered, has wrinkles where plaster can get in the scales. Having not been rendered well, the animal will attack itself and break and will never be perfect. Be also advised to make your casts for wax very big. In this way, your casts will not be too thick. They are done when the mold of both sides is done and when the animal has been removed from the mold.
